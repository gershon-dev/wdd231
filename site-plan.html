Section 01

Site Name

Chop Bar Chronicles is the chosen name for this project. In West African English, a chop bar is a casual, beloved roadside eatery — the heartbeat of neighbourhood food culture across Ghana, Nigeria, and beyond. Chronicles evokes storytelling, discovery, and an ongoing journey.

Together, the name signals an authentic, story-driven platform dedicated to celebrating African street food and local dining. It is culturally grounded, easy to remember, and distinct from generic food-review sites.

Proposed domain: chopbarchronicles.com  ·  The name resonates immediately with the target audience while remaining approachable and curious-sounding to newcomers discovering African cuisine for the first time.

Section 02

Site Purpose

Chop Bar Chronicles is a discovery and storytelling platform dedicated to African street food culture. The site profiles iconic dishes, local vendors, regional food traditions, and must-visit chop bars across the African continent.

Visitors can browse featured dishes with descriptions and cultural context, explore a curated vendor spotlight section, and submit their own local recommendations through a contact/recommendation form. The site also provides a newsletter sign-up for weekly food stories.

Core value proposition: authentic, community-contributed food discovery — bridging curious food travellers, diaspora communities, and local enthusiasts through the shared language of street food.

Section 03

Scenarios

The following scenarios represent questions real visitors from the target audience might ask when arriving at the site. They guide content decisions and information architecture.

  • I'm travelling to Accra next month — where can I find the best kelewele and waakye spots that locals actually go to?
  • I grew up eating suya in Lagos and now live in London. Are there any vendor stories or recipes that will remind me of home?
  • I want to recommend my grandmother's chop bar in Kumasi — how do I submit it to be featured on the site?
  • What are the most iconic street foods in East Africa, and how do they differ from West African dishes?

Section 05

Typography

Three complementary typefaces are selected to create an editorial, warm, and culturally rich voice across the site.

Font 1  ·  DM Serif Display  ·  Google Fonts
Chop Bar Chronicles
Taste the Story
Used for: Site name/logo, hero headings, section titles, pull quotes. The italic variant adds elegance and warmth.
Font 2  ·  Syne  ·  Google Fonts
Featured Vendor  ·  Street Food Guide  ·  Submit a Spot
Used for: Navigation links, section labels, buttons, tag labels, UI elements. Bold weight (800) for maximum legibility at small sizes.
